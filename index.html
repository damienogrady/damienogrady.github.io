Temperature

Projected mean near-surface temperature and daily maximum/minimum temperatures changes from two CMIP5 climate models (GISS-E2-H and IPSL-CM5A-LR), applied a baseline climatology from Worldclim v2.1. The two models cover the upper and lower ranges of variability in CMIP5 model projections of temperature for Vanuatu, with GISS-E2-H representing lesser increases and IPSL-CM5A-LR representing greater increases in temperature.

Projections are available for the periods 2020-2039, 2040-2059, 2060-2079 and 2080-2099, and for emission scenarios RCP 2.6 (lower emissions) and RCP 8.5 (higher emissions).

Precipitation

Projected precipitation changes from two CMIP5 climate models (GISS-E2-H and IPSL-CM5A-LR), applied a baseline climatology from Worldclim v2.1. The two models cover the upper and lower ranges of variability in CMIP5 model projections of precipitation for Vanuatu, with GISS-E2-H representing dryer conditions and IPSL-CM5A-LR representing wetter conditions. The baseline Worldclim v2.1 climatology is also included to represent near-past conditions.

Projections are available for the periods 2020-2039, 2040-2059, 2060-2079 and 2080-2099, and for emission scenarios RCP 2.6 (lower emissions) and RCP 8.5 (higher emissions).

Climate projections are derived from climate models that have limitations. Uncertainties at regional and local scales over the next decade are strongly influenced by natural variability, which is difficult to predict. Global climate models (GCMs) have coarse resolution and can provide useful climate projections over the next two decades and beyond at global and continental scales. However, GCMs cannot adequately represent weather-scale (1–10 km) phenomena, so downscaling methods have been used. The numerical precision of these data must not be confused with accuracy; the downscaled projections are plausible, rather than precise. Where possible, uncertainties have been estimated and confidence ratings have been provided.
